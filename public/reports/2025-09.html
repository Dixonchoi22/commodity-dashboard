Trend Analysis Summary

The EU Food HICP experienced a minor month-on-month decline in September, dropping to 147.84. This deceleration from the August peak suggests a temporary easing of immediate consumer price pressure, despite the overall YoY inflation remaining persistent at +3.0% due to sustained elevated costs in the supply chain.

Executive Summary Highlights

Extreme Price Risk:

Hazelnuts Turkey saw an extreme rise (+135.3% YoY), signaling severe risk in confectionery. Beef EU is also high risk at +38.7% YoY.

Major Price Opportunity:

Procurement Actions & Strategy

High Risks

  • •Hazelnuts, Dried Apricots, and Brazil Nuts show extreme YoY inflation (+63% to +135%) requiring immediate hedging or product reformulation to mitigate confectionery cost shock.
  • •Fish & Seafood remains highly volatile (Haddock +70% YoY), demanding contract flexibility and seeking alternative whitefish supply.
  • •Meat & Poultry faces upward pressure (Beef EU/UK +34% to +38% YoY), indicating structural supply tightness that requires longer-term fixed price negotiations.

Strategic Opportunities

  • •Maximize coverage for deflationary commodities: Potato processing EU (-60.0%), Onion India (-52.2%), Orange Juice US (-49.9%), and Sugar ICE #11 New York (-31.9%).
  • •Leverage stable Softs markets (Cocoa is relatively flat YoY) for strategic spot purchases rather than long-term commitments.
  • •Review dairy contracts: Massive deflation in Milk and Butter prices presents immediate opportunities for price negotiation across the EU supply base.

Tactical Recommendations

  • •Dairy & Packaging: Initiate tenders immediately to capitalize on the steep recent MoM price drops (e.g., Butter EU -17.7%, Milk UK -21.7%).
  • •Grains & Feed: Monitor Maize CBOT (up 9.6% MoM) closely; consider forward buys to lock in current prices before this recent spike passes through the supply chain.
  • •Review sourcing strategies for high-risk imported goods (Nuts) to diversify away from climate/geopolitical dependent single-country suppliers.
