Trend Analysis Summary

The EU Food HICP index (CP01) continued to climb through 2025, reaching 148.80 by December (latest published by Eurostat). YoY inflation has moderated from +3.4% mid-2025 to roughly +2.0% by year-end. The cooling headline rate masks severe month-on-month spikes in specific categories — Egg US (+106.2% MoM, Easter demand shock), Tomato EU (+48.3% MoM, Spanish supply shortage), Lamb UK (+15.1% MoM, Easter & Ramadan).

Executive Summary Highlights

Extreme price risk

Egg US +106.2% MoM and Tomato EU +48.3% MoM / +42.0% YoY on Spanish supply shortage. Lamb UK +15.1% MoM / +22.0% YoY driven by Easter & Ramadan demand.

Major opportunity

Potato processing EU -73.9% YoY, Milk EU -73.1% YoY, Cocoa ICE London -61.5% YoY, Butter EU -42.5% YoY — window for long-term contracts.

Energy spillover

Crude oil volatility from the Middle East has pushed vegetable oils higher: Soyabean Oil US +55.5% YoY, Rapeseed Oil Canada +41.2% YoY, Palm Oil EU +4.2% YoY.
